If there was one word I'd use to describe the beautifully vibrant and multicultural city that is Melbourne, it'd be coffee. Dark brown, caffeine-infused, broiling hot coffee. I often like to joke that it's the drink that powers Melbourne.

[[MORE]]

Today, there are more cafés than actual stores in the city. Every suburb I visit isn't a long black short of serving coffee, and almost everyone I meet isn't at least a self-certified coffee connoisseur. In other words, the coffee industry is big, and a potential platform waiting to be “Uber-ized” by Australia's startup cult.

So in typical “I wish there was X” fashion, I’d say there’s a lot that can be done on top of Melbourne’s coffee culture. For me at least, I wish there was a way to peek at a café’s menu before ordering. And moreover, because I’m usually (always) in rush, I wish there was a way to pre-order a cup before I even talk to a barista.

And then I found Hey You.

Go check out their app— what they offer is exactly what Melbourne’s coffee industry needs. Hey You aims to make every café around you accessible online. It’s pretty cool, actually.

Through the Hey You app, you can browse for cafés around you and view full menus. The app also works as a loyalty-rewards program, which keeps track of how many times you've been to a café then automatically rewards you for visits. In other words, it's a really useful app for the intrepid coffee-lover.

But that's not even the best part: Hey You lets you pre-order anything through their app. Once you’ve found a café, you can pre-purchase what you want from the menu— be it coffee, tea or even a full breakfast— and then pick it up once you arrive. It’s fast, cashless, and convenient. Not to mention— an incredibly valuable business idea.

In fact, that’s the reason Hey You started out in the first place. Back then, they were known as Beat The Q and it was a website that allowed users to quickly buy coffee before arriving at a café. Because this was back in 2011, they were one of the first and only businesses that monetised coffee pre-ordering— way before fin-tech companies like Square or even coffee-chains like Starbucks had even implemented it.

So, how does it all work? From what I can gather in their press releases, Hey You’s business runs entirely on the commission model. And on the legal side of things, they’re operating as an Australian private company (I’ve written about what this means, a few blog posts ago).

On the commission model: it works a little bit like how fin-tech companies do it. In exchange for $33 a month, Hey You PTY LTD provides cafés with a queueing system and integrates them into their network of registered cafés. Then for every purchase done through the app, Hey You takes a small percentage, and cafés pay for all the credit card transaction fees.

All in all, it’s a really clever way of managing cost and revenue. But what really piqued my interest, was their technical implementation. (If you’ll allow me to geek out a bit here...)

The entire system runs on Amazon’s scalable cloud infrastructure. This means that they can easily handle a huge spike in user signups (from tens to the thousands) and have the monthly fee of a single café cover the cost.

This cloud infrastructure is usually reserved for big data tech companies like Facebook or Dropbox. But Hey You’s engineering team was clever enough to use it early, which means very little money is burned at managing servers and software.

That means Hey You maximises revenue not just externally through their commission model, but also internally thanks to their engineering team. This is what a lot of fledging startups need to take advantage of— especially if they’re trying to enter the tech space.

Engineering aside, I see an opportunity for Hey You to scale into fast-food outlets and other chains. There are still queues in every Grill’d or San Churro I walk into, waiting to be replaced by a pre-ordering system. Hey You would have to move fast however— and as competitors crop up alongside them— it won’t be long until big food franchises develop their own.

Since 2011, Hey You has reached over 500,000 users, partnered with over 56,000 cafés around Australia (and New Zealand) and has processed an average of over 400,000 transactions per month. And after Westpac’s investment and partnership, they’re now a Series A startup valued at just over $5 million dollars.

The secret to Hey You’s success isn’t just because their idea is so original. It’s because they took advantage of an existing industry and made it even better. And for us coffee-loving Melbournites— we’ll never have to wait in line for our coffee, ever again.
