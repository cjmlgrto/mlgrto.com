  • I'm one part student, one part designer and one part writer.

    I'm CJ Melegrito. I often refer to myself as a Gestalt-Ingenieur. Translated from German, it literally means "Shape-Engineer", and I like to design and solve problems in my spare time. I also write code and play piano and the guitar whenever I have the chance.

    When I'm not writing for my site, I'm writing over at The Industry. Elsewhere on the web, I post some of my designs up at Dribbble, take pictures as an amateur photographer on Flickr, or even make some YouTube videos.

    I also love and serve the Lord God, as I am a Catholic, and I love Jesus as he is my savior.

    If you're wondering what I sit in front of whenever I blog, I have an amazing 27" iMac, an Apple Wireless Keyboard, an Apple Magic Mouse, an iPhone and an external 1TB hard drive, on which I use Time Machine to backup my important files with.

    About This site

    The Backlog is my own personal, online web-column. Here, I share some of my experiences, opinions, thoughts, some interesting links from elsewhere on the internet, and perhaps, I post a photo or two occasionally.

    The phrase "Backlog" comes from a conjugation of the phrases back-end and weblog. I didn't really know then that the word Backlog actually meant "an accumulation of something". It must've been some kind of strange, yet interesting, coincidence.

    The fonts I use on this site are modified versions of FF Tisa Web Pro and Cheltenham Bold, which is a font used, if you've seen it, in the mobile app version of The New York Times.
