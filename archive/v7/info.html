is striving to combine science and art to inspire, solve problems, and build a better world.

He is a multi-disciplinary designer, creating interfaces, icons and websites for digital systems. Occasionally, his work also extends to writing and educating on design theory, and producing videos.

In 2010, he was inducted as a member of the AGDA, and a year later, started writing as a design journalist for The Industry. In 2013, he collaborated together with Bohemian Coding, creators of the Apple Design Award-winning Sketch for Mac.

He lives in Melbourne, Australia and is currently studying a major in Computer Science (with a minor in Physics) under a specialised honors degree at Monash University.

He believes that science and art are one and the same: equal parts discovery and creativity.

About the blog

If you're into gadgets, startups, software development, design theory, great movies, and the intersection between science and liberal arts— then you've come to the right place.

His blog has been a 6-year-long-running journal of thoughts on the above, ideas, and updates to his work and projects. It's his humble abode on the web— feel free to make yourself at home.
