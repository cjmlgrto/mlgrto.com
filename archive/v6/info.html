CJ Melegrito is in three parts a student, a writer, and a designer.

Curious, light-hearted, and willing to learn, CJ Melegrito is a true student. He often refers to himself as a Gestalt Ingenieur which, literally translated from German, means Shape-Engineer. He believes that every challenge is malleable and he can impose a shape upon them through design. He’s also a member of the Australian Graphic Design Association, and an Apple fan.

Etymology of The Backlog

The Backlog is CJ Melegrito’s home on the internet. In other words, it’s a place for him to write about his thoughts, give off his opinion, and rant about things.

This site is running on Tumblr, hosted by Amazon S3, and was built with Espresso 2 on a Mac (though, that doesn’t mean you can’t build a great website on its counterpart). The main typeface used on this site is Elena, designed by Nicole Dotin, available at the Process Type Foundry.

As for the term The Backlog, it was originally meant to be a combination of the phrases back-end and weblog. It was just a crazy coincidence that the word backlog meant a collection of things.
